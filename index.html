Lunch Stop Options (pick one) Exits 290-312 · ~11:30 AM-12:30 PM
Real food, not gas station. Three strong options right off I-75.

Option A: Champy's Fried Chicken — 6515 Ringgold Rd, East Ridge, TN (Exit 1 TN, <2 min off highway). Sat 10:30AM-midnight. Legendary Chattanooga fried chicken, catfish, Southern sides. $9-14. Zero detour — literally at the exit. Dairy-free: chicken batter is flour-based, sides like beans/slaw/greens work. Top pick if timing works (~12:30 PM).

Option B: North Indian Flame — 115 Travelers Pathway, Calhoun, GA (Exit 312, <2 min off highway). Sat opens 10AM. Surprisingly legit Indian restaurant in small-town Georgia. Tandoori, curries, biryani, naan. $10-18. Dairy-free: excellent — specify "no cream, no ghee." Dal, chana masala, tandoori chicken all work. Top pick if you want something more adventurous (~11:30 AM).

Option C: El Dorado Salvadorian & Mexican — 910 Joe Frank Harris Pkwy, Cartersville, GA (Exit 290, ~5 min off highway). Sat 11AM-10PM. Pupusas, tamales, burritos. $8-15. Dairy-free: high — Salvadorian pupusas are naturally dairy-free (corn masa + beans/meat). Earliest option (~11:15 AM).

Note: No pork constraint — Champy's has chicken/catfish, Indian Flame has all non-pork options, El Dorado has beef/chicken.

🏔️ Townsend + Low-Effort Smokies

Drive: ~75-90 min from Airbnb. Take US-321 S to Townsend ("Peaceful Side of the Smokies").

Route sequence:

  1. Foothills Parkway West — 17-mile scenic drive, Look Rock observation tower (0.8 mi RT hike, 360° views). 50 min from Airbnb.
  2. Meigs Falls — Visible from a roadside pullout on Little River Road. Zero hiking. 1 hr 15 min from Airbnb.
  3. Sugarlands Visitor Center — Restrooms, bookshop, exhibits. 1 hr from Airbnb.
  4. Sugarlands Valley Nature Trail — 0.5 mi paved, flat, ADA accessible. The only fully accessible trail in the park. Stroller-friendly. Benches along the way.
  5. Cataract Falls — 0.7 mi RT from Sugarlands. Easy, 32 ft elevation gain. Small 40-ft waterfall. Most dramatic after rain.
  6. Cades Cove (optional, adds 2-4 hrs) — 11-mile one-way scenic loop. 20 mph speed limit. Wildlife (deer almost certain, black bears possible, wild turkeys). Cable Mill stop with historic buildings, restrooms. Warning: once you start, there is no shortcut out. Commit or skip. With kids, budget 3-4 hours minimum.

Parking: $5/day tag required (buy at recreation.gov, must print, match license plate). Under 15 min stops don't need one.

Closed: Laurel Falls Trail — closed for rehabilitation. Don't plan around it.

Best version: Foothills Parkway + Meigs Falls + Sugarlands + Cataract Falls. Skip Cades Cove — with the Shatner show at 7 PM, you need to be back at the Airbnb by 4-5 PM for downtime.

🔬 Oak Ridge — The Secret City

Drive: ~35 min from Airbnb. I-40 W to Oak Ridge.

Recommended sequence:

  1. AMSE (American Museum of Science & Energy) — 115 E Main St. Sun 1-5 PM. Adults $10, Youth $5, under 5 free. Hands-on science + energy exhibits. Start here.
  2. Manhattan Project Visitor Center — Inside Children's Museum at 461 W Outer Dr. Sun 1-4 PM. Closed Monday. NPS rangers, oral histories, the "Secret City" story.
  3. Children's Museum of Oak Ridge — Same building (461 W Outer Dr). Sun 1-4 PM. Adults $10, Kids $8. Hands-on backup if attention wobbles.
  4. K-25 History Center — 652 Enrichment St. Sun 1-5 PM. Free. The uranium enrichment plant story. Older-kid appropriate.

Note: Sunday hours start at 1 PM for most Oak Ridge venues. Don't rush out early — have a leisurely Knoxville morning, then head to Oak Ridge after lunch. Realistically, pick two of the four — AMSE alone can eat 1.5-2 hrs with hands-on kids, and you only have until 4-5 PM.

Also open Monday: AMSE (9-5). Others closed Monday. So if you want to split: do Manhattan Project + Children's Museum Sunday, AMSE Monday morning.

🎢 Ober Mountain + Gatlinburg

Drive: ~55 min from Airbnb to Ober base station (1001 Parkway, Gatlinburg).

Smart logistics: Park at Gatlinburg Welcome Center (1011 Banner Rd) and take the free trolley into downtown. Saves parking anxiety.

Ober Tram: 2.1-mile ride, 10 min each way, departs every 20 min. $29/person (5+), under 5 free. Spring Fun Pass $37 (includes most mountaintop attractions). Family of 4: ~$116-148.

At the top: Tennessee Flyer alpine coaster (open in April — separate charge or Adventure Pass), indoor ice skating, scenic chairlift, Wildlife Encounter.

Food at top: Seasons of Ober (full service), Ober Mountain Cafe (burgers/pretzels), Fiddle & Pick BBQ, Buzzin' Bear (coffee + ice cream).

Anakeesta note: Partially open — Ridge Rambler only (scenic truck ride), both coasters work, but Chondola is closed. $35 adults / $23 kids. Only worth combining if you're already in Gatlinburg and have set expectations.

Buy tram tickets online in advance: tickets.obermountain.com

⚠️ Shatner timing: 55 min drive back from Gatlinburg. To be at the Airbnb by 4:30 for downtime, you need to leave Gatlinburg by 3:15 PM. Plan your time at the top accordingly — don't start the alpine coaster at 2:45.

Nature & Outdoors

Easy-access, low-effort, high-reward
Seven Islands Birding Park
Peaceful
Tennessee's only state birding park. 1.2-mile paved accessible trail to a French Broad River overlook. 200+ species documented. Mid-April brings migrating warblers and Indigo Buntings. Merlin will light up here.
📍 2809 Kelly Ln, Kodak · 25 min 🕐 Dawn to dusk · Free
FacilitiesPortable restrooms only (no flush). No drinking water — bring your own. Free parking at Bluebird Barn. Trails1.2-mi paved ADA trail + 8 mi natural trails (easy to moderate) April birdsWarblers, Indigo Buntings, Yellow-breasted Chats, Northern Bobwhite, Wild Turkey, herons, swallows
💡 Best early morning for birding activity. But even midday, the landscape is beautiful — river, grassland, forest edge.

Factory tour (Mon only!) + free scoop of ice cream. 45-min guided tour, tours on the hour, last tour 3 PM.

Address: 806 E Madison Ave, Athens, TN 37303

Website: mayfielddairy.com

Monday hours: 10 AM – 5 PM. Tours available Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri. Last tour 3 PM.

Cost: Adults $8.50, Kids (4-15) $6.50, under 3 free + $1.50 ice cream → Family of 4: ~$32

Tour: 45 min guided factory tour + free scoop. Kids love seeing how ice cream is made.

Why Monday: Tours don't run Saturday or Sunday. Monday is the ONLY day this works for your trip.

The Lost Sea — Sweetwater Exit 60 · 12 min off I-75 · ~55 mi from Knox
If you didn't do this Saturday — America's largest underground lake. 75-min cave tour + boat ride. Open Mon 9:30-5.

Monday hours: 9:30 AM – 5:00 PM, tours every 30 min.

Cost: Adults $29.95, Kids $18.95 → ~$98 family

⚠️ Martha note: Boat ride on underground lake. Calm, flat, still water — but flag with Martha (nausea). Could be Andrew + kids while Martha browses the gift shop.

Cave temp: 58°F — bring jackets.

Time budget: 12 min off I-75 each way + 75 min tour = ~2 hrs total.

Red Clay State Historic Park Exit 20 · 20 min off I-75 · ~130 mi from Knox
Last Cherokee council ground before Trail of Tears. Replica village, Blue Hole spring, short trails. Free. Educational and beautiful.

Address: 1140 Red Clay Park Rd SW, Cleveland, TN 37311

Website: tnstateparks.com/parks/red-clay

Monday: Park grounds dawn to dusk. Visitor Center 1 PM – 4:30 PM. Free admission.

Trails: Blue Hole Trail (0.2 mi, easy, great for kids), Council of Trees Trail (1.7 mi, moderate, overlook tower).

Why go: Cherokee replica buildings, interpretive museum, Blue Hole spring (visually striking). Educational, age-appropriate for Eli and Rosie. Moving place.

Caveat: 20 min each way off I-75 + 1.5-2 hrs on-site = 2.5 hr commitment. Only if you have time. Visitor Center opens 1 PM Monday.

Lunch Stop Options (pick one) Exits 60-333 · ~12:00-1:00 PM
Real lunch on the way home. Three options at different points on the route.

Dairy-free: high — corn tortillas + meat + cilantro/onion. Same exit as Oakwood if you want to decide on arrival.
